Review of related literature

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (n.d.), The natural process of turning organic waste, such as leaves and food scraps, into a beneficial fertilizer that can improve soil and plants is known as composting. Composting merely expedites the decomposition process by creating the perfect environment for bacteria, fungi, and other decomposing organisms. Everything that grows eventually decomposes. By adding carbon to the soil, compost helps to enhance soil structure and gives plants nourishment.

According to Contributor, G. S. F. (2020), For this selected plant, the nutrients that it needs are nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Potassium is essential for the yield of the vegetable and it increases the growth and production of the okra plant. Phosphorus enhances the plant growth, production, and quality of the okra. Lastly, Nitrogen also helps with growth but too much of it can result in excessive growth. This shows that in enhancing the growth of the okra, it is crucial to be careful with adding nutrients to the okra plant.

Based on an article by Tanks Green Stuff (2019), Yard waste and leaves are used in an aerobic on-site composting method that results in a straightforward yet efficient kind of compost, while Vegan compost uses fruit & vegetable scraps. The needed materials for Yard waste and leaves compost are twigs, wood chips, leaves, and grass. Nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium are rich in fall leaves and vegetable scraps which helps give the Okra plant its needed nutrients.

According to Garden Tabs (2020), Vegan compost can also benefit the plant by contributing its nutrients to the soil and is generally odorless. Yard trash and leaves can be beneficial to the plant by providing it with the nutrients it needs, won't be damaging to the plant, and minimizing yard waste in an environmentally responsible method. In addition, vegan compost can also provide good drainage factors and suppress soil-borne diseases.

Compost made from yard waste and leftover paper product scraps is Treatment A. Control Set-up (set-up A)


The control set-up will be identified as Set up A. The setup will have a measure of 700 grams of regular soi or loam soil in order to compare with the two other experimental setups as the controlled setup. The experimental set-up will be identified as Set-up B. The setup will have a measure of 200 grams of composted mixture of food scraps.
